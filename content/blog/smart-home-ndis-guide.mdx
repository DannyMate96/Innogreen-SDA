# The Complete Guide to NDIS Smart Home Technology

Smart home technology has transformed the way people with disabilities live independently. If you're an NDIS participant, you may be eligible to access a range of assistive technologies through your plan — from voice-controlled lighting to automated doors.

## What is Smart Home Assistive Technology?

Smart home assistive technology refers to devices and systems that automate and simplify everyday tasks at home. For NDIS participants, these tools can make a meaningful difference in:

- **Daily independence** — controlling lights, blinds, and appliances without physical effort
- **Safety** — smart locks, video doorbells, and emergency alert systems
- **Communication** — voice-controlled devices that connect you to family and emergency services
- **Comfort** — automated climate control and sensory environment management

## How NDIS Funds Smart Home Tech

Smart home technology is typically funded under **Assistive Technology** in your NDIS plan. Depending on your needs, it may fall under:

- **Low-cost AT** (under $1,500) — purchased directly
- **Mid-cost AT** (under $15,000) — may require an OT assessment and quote
- **High-cost AT** — requires OT assessment, trial, and provider quote

An occupational therapist (OT) assessment is usually required to justify how the technology addresses your functional impairment.

## Common Smart Home Technologies Funded by NDIS

### Voice Control Systems
Control your lights, TV, fans, and more with simple voice commands. Compatible with Amazon Alexa, Google Home, and Apple HomeKit. Ideal for participants with limited mobility or fine motor challenges.

### Automated Doors and Locks
Smart locks allow keyless entry via PIN, fingerprint, or smartphone app — eliminating the challenge of physical keys. Automated sliding doors and garage openers provide wheelchair-friendly access.

### Video Intercoms and Doorbells
See and speak with visitors from anywhere in your home — or remotely via your phone. Particularly useful for participants who find it difficult to move quickly to answer the door.

### Motorised Blinds and Curtains
Automate the opening and closing of blinds via app, timer, or voice — managing natural light and privacy effortlessly.

### Environmental Control Units
A single device that lets you control multiple home systems (TV, fans, lights, air conditioning) through one interface — compatible with switch access, eye-gaze, and voice input.

## Getting Started

1. **Talk to your NDIS planner or LAC** about your interest in assistive technology
2. **Request an OT referral** — your OT will assess your needs and recommend appropriate devices
3. **Get a quote** from a registered NDIS assistive technology provider like Innogreen
4. **Trial the technology** before finalising (often recommended for higher-cost items)
5. **Purchase and install** with your NDIS plan funds
